VIST3A: Text-to-3D by Stitching a Multi-view Reconstruction Network to a Video Generator

Anonymous author

Rendered videos of generated 3DGS from our VIST3A method (Wan + AnySplat).

TL;DR: Text-to-3D scene latent generative model by unifying a video generative model with a foundational 3D model via model stitching and alignment.

Summary of our method

We address two limitations of existing 3D feedforward generative models leveraging 2D pre-trained models:

  • Limitation 1 — Decoder weakness. VAE decoder must learn 3D reconstruction almost from scratch, demanding extensive training and lagging behind rapidly improving 3D foundation models.
  • Limitation 2 — Misalignment. The generator (trained with diffusion/flow losses) and the VAE-style decoder are trained separately, so latents often fall out of the decoder’s domain.

Our approach (VIST3A). Instead of training a 3D decoder from scratch, we stitch a pretrained 3D model into the video VAE latent space, reusing strong 3D priors. We then apply direct reward finetuning to align generation with 3D quality and consistency—no 3D labels needed.

  • Plug-and-play stitching: search for a compatible layer in the 3D model to latent, connect via linear mapping. Thanks to good initialization from stitching, only light finetuning without additional data is needed to recover the original 3D model performance.
  • Direct 3D alignment: We strengthen the alignment by reward tuning the generative model so that the decoded 3D representations are high-quality and 3D-consistent.
